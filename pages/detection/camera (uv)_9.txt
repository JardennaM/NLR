Gepubliceerd op 14 sep. 2015Extending UAV Applicability to Power Line Inspection with Integrated Directional UV Detection  Nick Rymer, SWOSU, NIA, NASA LaRC  Andrew Moore, NASA LaRC  Matt Schubert, AMA, NASA LaRC  James Hubbard, UMD, NIAThis project demonstrated a cost efficient and safe means of detecting corona discharge using Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs). It harnesses the physics of high-voltage coronas to advance the utility of UAVs for power line inspection: coronas are the breakdown of the air around a high voltage conductor, which emit ultraviolet (UV) light and indicate potential defects in transmission lines and insulators. UV photosensors and electronics, mounted on brackets fabricated with a 3D printer, were attached to a UAV to point in 3 starboard directions: fore, side, and aft. An on-board micro-controller read detection events and created event alerts (via a speaker and three LEDs). The instrumented UAV was tested at the Electric Power Research Institute to verify and calibrate the system on a live 100kV corona source. As the UAV translated in front of the source, UV detection alerts fired according to aircraft’s angle to the corona. Finally, a tethered flight was performed with a lab corona generator indoors at the National Institute of Aerospace to verify the integration in a realistic operation.- Charlotte, NC and Hampton VA, USA July 2015Special thanks to:Fabio Bologna, Electric Power Research InstituteAndrew Phillips, Electric Power Research InstituteSharon Graves, NASA LaRCKathryn Guild, Media Solutions Branch, NASA Larc and Genex Systems Charles Hatfield, Media Solutions Branch, NASA Larc and Genex Systems
